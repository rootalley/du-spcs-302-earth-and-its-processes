Course Description

This course is designed to provide an overview and basic understanding of Earth Science and its many aspects (e.g. geologic time, plate tectonics, various types of Earth's physical feature,  and the various processes at work such as erosion, cycling of water, glaciation, etc.). The course also explore the atmosphere, weather, climate, and climate change. This is an asynchronous online course.

Prerequisite: SPCS 200

Course Objectives

Successful completion of this course will enable you to:

  • Briefly summarize the roughly 4.5 billion years' worth of Earth's formation, and the evolution of its structure and climate.
  • Discuss how Earth's current environment is a product of continuous change.
  • Describe the interactions between Earth's physical features, atmosphere, weather, and climate.
  • Describe the human impacts to the Earth's physical features, atmosphere, and climate.

Unit 1

Earth's Systems - Rocks & Minerals

Unit Objectives:

  • Explain how the Earth can be viewed as a system and a group of systems.
  • Compare the layers of Earth in terms of physical characteristics.
  • Discuss and give examples of physical properties of minerals.
  • Describe the geologic products that result from mechanical and chemical weathering.
  • Discuss and give examples of the physical properties of the three main categories of rocks and the processes that produced them.

Unit 3

Earth's Structure - Plate Tectonics, Earthquakes & Volcanoes

Unit Objectives:

  • Describe how continents are formed.
  • Describe tectonic theory.
  • Describe tectonic plate movement at a divergent boundary, a convergent boundary, and a transform boundary.
  • Describe the causes of earthquakes and describe methods of measuring the strength of an earthquake using one of three scales - Mercalli, Richter, and Moment Magnitude.
  • Describe processes for forming magma and volcanoes.
  • Describe the effect of volcanic eruptions on global climate.

Mountains, Weathering, Soil & Erosion

Unit 7

Atmosphere, Weather, Energy & Pollution

Unit Objectives:
