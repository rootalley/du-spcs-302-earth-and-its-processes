Welcome to SPCS 302 - Earth and Its Processes!

Earth and Its Processes is an introductory course to Earth Science. It is designed to provide an overview and basic understanding of Earth Science and its many aspects (e.g. geologic time, plate tectonics, various types of Earth's physical features), and the various processes at work such as erosion, cycling of water, glaciation, etc.. The course also explores the atmosphere, weather, climate, and climate change.

What Will I Learn?

Successful completion of this course will enable you to:

  • Summarize the roughly 4.5 billion years' worth of Earth's formation and evolution of its structure and climate.
  • Discuss how Earth's current environment is a product of continuous change.
  • Describe the interactions between Earth's physical features, atmosphere, weather, and climate.
  • Describe the human impacts to the Earth's physical features, atmosphere, and climate.

Course Format

This is an asynchronous course. This format provides a great deal of freedom for students to adopt study practices that work best for them. From experience, we can say that students will, on average, devote approximately 8–10 hours per week to this course. Your workload may vary somewhat from week to week and from student to student depending on prior experience, the nature of the topic being studied, and other factors.

This course is being delivered on Canvas and is organized into eight units. In each unit, you will find:

  • Assigned readings and videos
  • Discussion prompts, which require posting on this schedule: Make an initial post in each discussion by Wednesday at 11:59 p.m.; make required responses to others by Sunday at 11:59 p.m.
  • Quizzes
  • Writing assignments (Only some weeks)

Some units are busier than others, so be sure to review each module carefully so you can plan your workload for the week.

Unit 1 - Earth Systems - Rocks and Minerals

Welcome

Welcome to Unit 1: Earth Systems - Rocks and Minerals!

This unit will primarily focus on how the Earth was made, Earth's systems, and the rocks and minerals that make up the various layers of the Earth.

Things to complete this first week of class:

  1. "Introductions" Discussion
  2. Read: Chapter 1 - Earth Systems
  3. Quiz: Earth Systems
  4. Discussion: Earth Systems
  5. Read: Chapter 2 - Minerals
  6. Read: Chapter 3 - Rocks
  7. Discussion: Rocks and Minerals
  8. Watch: How the Earth Was Made
  9. Writing Assignment: How the Earth Was Made

Let me know if you have any questions or problems. Let’s get started!

Objectives

Successful completion of this unit will enable you to:

  • Explain how the Earth can be viewed as a system and a group of systems.
  • Compare the layers of Earth in terms of physical characteristics.
  • Discuss and give examples of physical properties of minerals.
  • Describe the geologic products that result from mechanical and chemical weathering.
  • Discuss and give examples of the physical properties of the three main categories of rocks and the processes that produced them.

Objectives

Successful completion of this unit will enable you to:

  • Describe how continents are formed.
  • Describe tectonic plate theory.
  • Describe tectonic plate movement at a divergent boundary, a convergent boundary, and a transform boundary.
  • Describe the causes of earthquakes and describe methods of measuring the strength of an earthquake using one of three scales: Mercalli, Richter, or Moment Magnitude.
  • Describe processes for forming magma and volcanoes.
  • Describe the effect of volcanic eruptions on global climate.

Objectives

Successful completion of this unit will enable you to:

Unit 6 - Deserts and Wind, Oceans and Coastlines

Welcome

Welcome to Unit 6: Deserts and Wind, Oceans and Coastlines!

This unit is focused on two very different elements of Earth's surface: deserts and oceans. You will discover that while deserts may share some similarities, they have developed for a variety of reasons and vary from the sand dunes of the Sahara to the scrubby dry regions of the American West. You will also discover that the existence of oceans has had a profound effect on the physical features of Earth, as well as its weather and climate.

Things to complete this week for class:

  1. Read: Chapter 14 - Deserts and Wind
  2. Quiz: Deserts and Wind
  3. Discussion: Deserts
  4. Read: Chapter 15 - Ocean Basins
  5. Read: Chapter 16 - Oceans and Coastlines
  6. Watch: Ocean Life Video Clips (7 videos)
  7. Writing Assignment: Ocean Life Video Clips
  8. Discussion: Oceans and Coastlines

Let me know if you have any questions or problems.

Objectives

Successful completion of this unit will enable you to:

  • Summarize the characteristics of deserts and explain the reasons for their existence.
  • Describe the effect of the oceans on global climate.
  • Discuss the location of the world's oceans in relation to the density of the Earth's crust and describe the faulting that occurs in the ocean basins.
  • Describe the Coriolis effect and the relationship between surface and deep water currents and climate.
